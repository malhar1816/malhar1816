Implementation of RFID-based Lab Inventory System

Published at

14 February 2023

This paper presents the design, implementation, and evaluation of a Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID)–based laboratory inventory management system aimed at addressing the chronic issues of equipment loss, misplacement, and manual tracking overhead in university research labs. A passive RFID reader array coupled via Arduino controllers to a central PC enables automated tagging and real-time identification of lab assets. The software stack includes a database backend accessible both locally and over a web interface, complete with search, add/delete, and security-alert modes. Through a two-mode operation ("inventory" and "security"), the system not only streamlines periodic audits reducing manual labor and errors but also triggers alarms when unauthorized or misplaced equipment is detected, enhancing both efficiency and safety. Experimental deployment demonstrated significant reductions in inventory-check times and near-zero tagging errors, validating the approach as a cost-effective solution for academic and small-scale industrial labs.

Passive RFID-Arduino Integration: Designed a low-cost interface using passive RFID readers connected to Arduino microcontrollers, enabling seamless tag reads within typical lab ranges without bulky equipment

The system operates in two modes: Inventory Mode automates scans and updates item locations in real-time, while Security Mode detects unauthorized tag movement and triggers alerts to enhance lab safety.

Web-Enabled Database Access: Implemented a browser-based front end allowing lab managers to remotely view, search (advanced query), add, or delete inventory records, improving flexibility and real-time oversight.

Structured Review Workflow: Introduced a clear data-entry and approval pipeline (tag registration → data verification → live monitoring) to minimize human error and maintain high data integrity.

Vendor & Component Management Evaluated off-the-shelf RFID readers, Arduino boards, and open-source database solutions to balance performance, cost, and ease of deployment in academic settings
